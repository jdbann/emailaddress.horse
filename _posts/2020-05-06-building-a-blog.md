Regular readers might recognise a complete rebuild of this place. It's maybe
the fourth time I've tried it in the last year. I must be pushing fifty or so
attempts at building a personal site since I first started in Frontpage.

The difference this time? I'm focusing on content.

I've been pretty excited about this great post: [Web Design in 4
minutes]. It reminded me of the
approach I take when building products: to focus on the riskiest part first. For
a personal blog, that's the posts themselves.

It's not about a complicated tagging system, an elaborate design, or a
fully-integrated marketing engine to fill social media with links and quotes.
It's about writing something a person wants to read. Then it's about making that
experience nicer.

So we have a simple site built with [Jekyll] and hosted on [GitHub Pages]. It
doesn't even use a Jekyll theme. It just shows posts written in markdown to
people visiting a website.

And from there I can write more posts. Very simple, very light ones. I can see
what I want to say, and I can see what people want to read. Then I can improve
on what matters.
