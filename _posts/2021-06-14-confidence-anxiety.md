Over the weekend, I finished off an idea I had been playing with for the [Tiny Library Jam](https://itch.io/jam/tiny-library-jam- "Tiny Library Jam on Itch.io"). In the last few weeks, my therapist explained what _anxiety_ was. It had been floating around for so long that I didn't realise it was a nameable thing.

The exciting realisation was that anxiety was all about my _perception_ of my actions, not whether or not they were valuable contributions.

So I present CONFIDENCE/ANXIETY, a simple hack for any RPG that uses a dice-roll for a skill-check or save:

[CONFIDENCE/ANXIETY on Itch.io](https://emailaddressdothorse.itch.io/confidenceanxiety "CONFIDENCE/ANXIETY on Itch.io")

![CONFIDENCE. When you want to do something which will require a check or save, roll before making your plan. Then come up with a plan which will achieve that result. Your character will execute this plan flawlessly and look impressive whilst doing it — but the end result of the check will be the same.](/uploads/confidence-2x.png "CONFIDENCE Rules")

![ANXIETY. When you want to do something which will require a check or save, follow the rules of your game. Your character will see the result as a failure. You will know that everyone has seen you fail even though they might not mention it — but the end result of the check will be the same.](/uploads/anxiety-2x.png "ANXIETY Rules")
