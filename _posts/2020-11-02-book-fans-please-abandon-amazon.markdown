title: Book fans, please abandon Amazon
date: 2020-11-02 09:56:00 Z
---

Two great options for book fans who want to get further away from Amazon.

First: [goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com) is a nice tool for tracking your reading, finding what others have read and looking for reviews or recommendations. It's owned by Amazon though, and provides a wealth of useful information. I'm liking [The Story Graph](https://thestorygraph.com/index.html) as an alternative.

It's largely the same thing, but an independent team is building it. I switched over last weekend and have been enjoying it. I'm not sure how niche you can go with the books but it found all my Dungeons & Dragons ones. Plus the stats it gives are an interesting if light look.

![My 'moods' from the Story Graph. Mostly adventurous, reflective and informative](/uploads/storygraph%20moods.png)

Second: a decent online shopping experience can make a big difference for an impulse purchase. I don't mind sending an email to a bookshop for something I've been thinking about for a while, but I'm not going to go through that for something which caught my eye and seemed interesting.

Add to that a lockdown where you can't browse shops all that easily and suddenly online shopping goes to the most streamlined experience. This very today (Monday 2nd November 2020) [Bookshop.org](https://uk.bookshop.org) launched in the UK. They provide a friendly online shopping interface for independent bookshops. They pay 30% of the listed price to either: a specific bookshop you select; or a pot of funds which will be shared across all the listed independent bookshops.

As ever, don't take my word, do your own research, and see [what they have to say themselves](https://uk.bookshop.org/pages/about). But they're a certified B-Corp and [this podcast episode](https://rework.fm/bookshop-around-the-corner/) explains the rest nicely.
