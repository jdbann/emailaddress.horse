Thanks for taking an interest! I've started running a book club on Discord. We
read and discuss indie RPGs (and become the bestest of friends).

In our first session, we discussed [Cairn](https://cairnrpg.com) and it was so
great to hear such a range of experiences in gaming. A small group of players,
GMs, creators and writers brought up some interesting details about emergent
mechanics and influences.

I'm biased of course, but I think it was great and that you'd have a great time
if you joined in!

## What’s the main idea?

Games are fun. They’re also interesting. Let’s talk about what we find fun or
interesting. That’s all there is to it. Like-minded people being enthusiastic
about games!

## What will we do?

**Introductions** - Say hi, share your name and pronouns and a little about what
brings you to the group.

**A note on safety tools** - We’re probably not getting into anything too spicy,
but that’s just my opinion so we have some safety tools to make sure everyone
can take part freely.

**Initial thoughts on the game** - We’ll go round everyone to share a few brief
thoughts before we get into looser discussion - this will help identify some
topics and themes we’d like to return to. I’ll invite every attendee to have
some time to share their thoughts, but no-one is obliged to speak and we’re just
as happy to have listeners.

**Open discussion** - At this stage, we should be eager to get into some greater
back and forth on what we shared in our initial thoughts. Get stuck in with the
discussion, but be polite and considerate - if you've noticed someone wants to
add something help make space for them.

Some good things to do would be:
- Draw comparisons to similar ideas in other games
- Share your experience of playing other games and how these mechanics might
  have changed things
- Draw ideas together from other things we’ve been discussing
