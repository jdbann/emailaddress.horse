Games

To highlight my compulsion to collect indie games, and my shame at taking so long to read and play them, here is my collection and the embarrassing data.

12 Years

A ‘solo+ rpg’ exploring generated landscapes and dungeons against a twelve-year deadline. There’s plenty to do though, and time behaves strangely in this world. Great at showing rather than telling and invites the player to find their own balance between rules-driven and narrative-driven play.

View game · Bought on 24th May 2021 · Read on 6th June 2021

Aether Operations

Spirit, brain, bone and meat. Almost seems like a set of stats, but they’re the realms you will explore. I would say this game is unplayable but in one of those elusive and tantalising ways. It’d be a lot of fun to try!

Cairn

Lightweight OSR system. Heavy on principles for play but quite light on setting and tone. Leans more to combat and simple mechanics for saving throws and doesn’t offer many thoughts on adventuring. Probably wont try to play.

View game · Bought on 25th May 2021 · Read on 5th June 2021

Mausritter

Come be a mouse with me and we’ll explore villages in tree stumps and fight off marauding cats. I love this game. It achieves a really simple gameplay experience and is one of those worlds which is relatable enough to freely play in without needing any further context. Just pretend to be tiny in your garden!

Metal Queens ov Skull Mountain

Simple mechanics and a whole mass of dark metal intensity in such an engaging way. I don’t think I could play this game, but my mind had images of late 2000s CGI music videos as I read it which hasn’t happened with any other game!
