How to get around HEY.com’s email tracking restrictions

15th June 2020

Don’t.

I’m sure someone will come up with a way to do this. The arms race will step up a level and the marketing industry will get more aggressive.

But if you’re a developer reading this, take a moment to think about other ways to handle this shift. The ‘tracking restrictions’ are just a way to protect users from spying practices.

Do something interesting instead—something that considers the recipients as your users just as much as the senders.

Offer a link which people can click to say they found the email interesting.

Start a push for adding tracking callbacks to email headers which email clients can choose to implement.

Ask if the emails themselves are even necessary, and if there is a different way to promote yourself.

It’s not very often that something shakes such a widespread system. Don’t react to patch the issue, plan for the exciting new future opportunities instead.
