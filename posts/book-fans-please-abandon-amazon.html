Book fans, please abandon Amazon

2nd November 2020

Two great options for book fans who want to get further away from Amazon.

First: goodreads is a nice tool for tracking your reading, finding what others have read and looking for reviews or recommendations. It’s owned by Amazon though, and provides a wealth of useful information. I’m liking The Story Graph as an alternative.

It’s largely the same thing, but an independent team is building it. I switched over last weekend and have been enjoying it. I’m not sure how niche you can go with the books but it found all my Dungeons & Dragons ones. Plus the stats it gives are an interesting if light look.

Second: a decent online shopping experience can make a big difference for an impulse purchase. I don’t mind sending an email to a bookshop for something I’ve been thinking about for a while, but I’m not going to go through that for something which caught my eye and seemed interesting.

Add to that a lockdown where you can’t browse shops all that easily and suddenly online shopping goes to the most streamlined experience. This very today (Monday 2nd November 2020) Bookshop.org launched in the UK. They provide a friendly online shopping interface for independent bookshops. They pay 30% of the listed price to either: a specific bookshop you select; or a pot of funds which will be shared across all the listed independent bookshops.

As ever, don’t take my word, do your own research, and see what they have to say themselves. But they’re a certified B-Corp and this podcast episode explains the rest nicely.
