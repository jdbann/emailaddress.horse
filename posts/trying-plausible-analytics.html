Trying Plausible.io for analytics

6th May 2020 | my setup

I’ve signed up for a trial of Plausible.io and installed the snippet here. I’ve been quite averse to analytics and tracking. Usually because it completely overreaches and ties your activity on one site to that on another. Then packages all that up to add extra value to targeted advertising opportunities.

Plausible doesn’t. It tracks visits, basic device info and country of origin. Nothing personally identifiable, and nothing that can connect one session to another. It’s perfect for just finding out how many people viewed a post.

You can view this site’s analytics here.

I’ll be using it to see if any posts do well, and to test out the service for use in other side projects which might have more of an audience.
