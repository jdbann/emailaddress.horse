A game is brewing

26th June 2021

There’s a game brewing in the back of my head. A game where you protect your village, your community. Adventures come from threats and opportunities for your settlement, to protect and support their resources. The intention is to prosper and thrive, but not needlessly grow and excessively consume.

The idea is dancing between a few games I admire. The simplicity of Mausritter that gives enough mechanical crunch but stands back for characterful roleplay. The community focus and tone of Stonetop that finds modest ambitions and a focus on how you help those who don’t adventure. The character creation options of Down We Go which does away with complex classes whilst still giving some sense of archetypes and play styles.

Loosely, I think character abilities and options are governed by your settlement. The settlement has resources it uses to survive, and over time those resources are put at risk. Perhaps a natural event disrupts them, or maybe more malicious interruptions from unfriendly factions. Adventures are emergent from the needs of the settlement and the world is a dangerous place.
