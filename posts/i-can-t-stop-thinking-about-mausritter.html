I can’t stop thinking about Mausritter

10th June 2021

It’s a huge and dangerous world out there, and it does not look kindly on a small mouse. But if you are very brave and very clever and just a bit lucky, you might be able to survive. And if you survive long enough, you might even become a hero amongst mice.

Mausritter a simple RPG about mice having adventures and it lives in my head now. This post is an attempt to understand how it got there and why I’m not letting it leave.

Everything is about what is happening now.

You don’t make skill checks which succeed or fail - you make saves. So action always moves forwards, you just have to hope your mouse survives.

You don’t have a complicated character. You get a few interesting prompts: a birthsign with an associated disposition; some phsyical characteristics; and a background which gives you two items. Very limited choices. The interesting thing about a character is what they do in this game, not what they did.

You keep track of time. Skipping an opportunity to rest, or wasting time in an adventure site, has ramifications. Nothing hugely punishing, but the decision you make affect you later. So your decisions count.

Mausritter is not unique in this regard, a lot of other OSR games use similar mechanics. But this game has such a focused way of delivering this sense of presence. The design, layout, writing and art all bring it back to this moment. Even the random tables give you things to respond to immediately, rather than referring back to lore (you skimmed) in other parts of the book.

I don’t think this captures everything I love about this game. But I do love it.
